About Healyis

Medicine is too important to be locked behind jargon. We're a team of clinicians on a mission to change that — one disease at a time.

Our Mission

Healyis was founded on a simple belief: every person deserves to understand their own health. Whether you've just received a diagnosis, are caring for a loved one, or are simply curious about how the body works — you deserve clear, honest, evidence-based information.

We saw the gap between what patients needed and what they could find online — a landscape full of oversimplified summaries, outdated articles, and health misinformation. Healyis is our answer.

Every article on this site is written or reviewed by a qualified clinician, cited to peer-reviewed literature, and designed to build genuine understanding — not just deliver a list of bullet points.

Our Core Values

Accuracy First

Every claim is cited to peer-reviewed evidence. We update articles when guidelines change. If we're uncertain, we say so.

Radical Accessibility

Medical knowledge should not be gated by education, income, or language. We write clearly for a general audience — without dumbing it down.

Editorial Independence

We accept no pharmaceutical advertising or sponsored content. Our recommendations are driven purely by evidence and clinical judgement.

Patient Partnership

We design our tools — quizzes, checklists, action plans — to help patients have better conversations with their doctors, not to replace those conversations.

Editorial Policy

Writing & Review Process

All articles are drafted by a qualified clinician with relevant specialty training, then reviewed by at least one additional physician before publication. We follow JAMA and BMJ reporting standards for clinical content.

Sources & Citations

We prioritise systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and randomised controlled trials from reputable journals (NEJM, The Lancet, JAMA, BMJ). We also reference official guidelines from the WHO, CDC, AHA, ADA, GINA, and other recognised bodies.

Update Policy

Medicine evolves rapidly. We review all articles when major new guidelines are published, and at minimum annually. Updated articles display a "Reviewed" date alongside the original publication date.

Medical Disclaimer

All content on Healyis is for educational and informational purposes only. It does not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations. Always consult a qualified, licensed healthcare professional before making any health-related decision.
